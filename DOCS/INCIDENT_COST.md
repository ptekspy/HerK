# 🚀 Company #3

## **IncidentCost** (working name)

### One-Line Pitch

> IncidentCost quantifies the true financial impact of engineering incidents in real time.

---

# 🧠 The Problem

Engineering teams track:

* Uptime
* Error rates
* Incident counts
* MTTR

But they do **not** track:

> What each incident actually cost the business.

So:

* Leadership underestimates impact.
* Postmortems lack financial weight.
* Platform teams struggle to justify investment.
* Reliability improvements don’t get funded.

Outages are treated emotionally, not economically.

---

# 💥 The Cost (Hidden but Massive)

Every incident impacts:

* Revenue (direct downtime loss)
* Conversion rates
* SLA penalties
* Support load
* Churn risk
* Reputation

But companies rarely connect engineering failures to financial numbers.

So engineering risk remains abstract.

---

# 🛠 The Solution

IncidentCost connects:

* Error monitoring (Sentry, Datadog)
* Status pages
* Deployment logs
* Revenue metrics (Stripe, billing systems)
* Traffic analytics

It then:

1. Detects incident windows.
2. Calculates affected revenue.
3. Models churn risk exposure.
4. Estimates SLA liability.
5. Produces a financial impact report automatically.

Postmortems become:

> “This outage cost £73,400.”

Not:

“Service degradation occurred.”

---

# 🔍 What It Actually Does (MVP)

Core integrations:

* Stripe (revenue + subscription value)

Engine:

* Estimate lost revenue during downtime.

Dashboard:

* Incident list
